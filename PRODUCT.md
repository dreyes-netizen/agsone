# Product

## Register

product

## Users

AGS (Alliance Global Solutions) employees, managers, and HR admins. Used daily during working hours on desktop, occasionally on mobile. Employees check points, browse the marketplace, and interact with the feed. Managers award points to their teams. HR admins manage everything — employees, rewards, reports, audit logs.

## Product Purpose

AGS One is an internal employee engagement and rewards platform. It gamifies workplace recognition: employees earn points through performance, attendance, and peer recognition, then spend them in the rewards marketplace. It replaces generic HR tools with something employees actually want to open — combining a social feed, a points economy, minigames, food ordering, and HR workflows under one roof.

## Brand Personality

Professional, engaging, human. Trustworthy enough for HR workflows, warm enough that employees check it daily by choice. The product should feel like a well-made internal tool — not a startup SaaS, not legacy enterprise software.

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS (Notion/Linear/Vercel aesthetic)**: No indigo-heavy ultra-minimal startup look. AGS One is a workplace product for a BPO company, not a dev tool.
- **Heavy enterprise (SAP/Workday/Oracle)**: No dark-gray tables, no accordion menus, no "HR portal" energy. Should feel lighter and more human.

## Design Principles

1. **Familiar, not flashy** — Employees shouldn't need a tutorial. Patterns should be obvious on first use; delight lives in the details, not in disruption.
2. **Data at a glance** — Points balances, leaderboards, and status are the product. Numbers and badges should be immediately scannable without hunting.
3. **Earned, not plastered** — Gamification elements (points, badges, levels) should feel meaningful and contextual, not decorative noise on every screen.
4. **HR-grade reliability** — Admin tools handle real data: payroll-adjacent points, disciplinary records, medical requests. Visual confidence matters. No ambiguity about state.
5. **One platform, many contexts** — The same shell hosts a social feed, a food board, minigames, and compliance reports. Each section should feel native to its purpose while sharing a coherent visual language.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA as baseline (already heavily improved throughout this session). Keyboard navigation, screen reader support, and focus-visible rings are implemented across all pages. `prefers-reduced-motion` should be respected for any animations added going forward.
